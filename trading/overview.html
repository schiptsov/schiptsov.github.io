#+TITLE overview

Depending on which market one have to put emphasis on certain patterns which may emerge from the apparent chaos (incomprehensible complexity)

Depending on which trend, one has to look at particular parts of the graph, such as lower or upper edges of ranges.

Basically, the most powerful patterns are Double Top in a bull (signalling a possible major reversal) and Higher Low, signalling “resuning” an uptrend after a failed, false reversal.

False break outs are in the bear markets are fast and violent, fueled by hopium of amateurs and FOMO. They usually create nice, long wicks.

The end of a major panic selloff is also a wick. It is called “to catch a falling knife”. A bounces (reversals) are fast and powerful, just like “physical” bounces of a ball.

If there is no bounce this is not a local “bottom”.

All the sharp moves and market “runs” are due to particularly strong emptions of fear of a loss, or Fear Of Missing Out which is the main force behind hopium fuelled rallies.

Everything sounds logical and reasonable. The only problems is that these advices are advices of a coach outside of a boximg ring. Trading is taking an actual fight inside a ring - a completely different experience from studying the theory and patterns. Another comparison is a theoretical training of a pilot versus actual flight, or the same like participating in a Moto Grand Prix. Or in a war on actual front lines.

This is why sellers of books and few day curses are just boxing coaches and they make consistently more money safely and easily. No coach is going inside a ring or actually ride that suberbike.
