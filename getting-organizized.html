When one begins to thing about “how to be productive” and start to look at planning software - it is too late and one is already got fucked.

Anyway, the main universal vehicle of any planning is a plain old sorted list – sorted by priorities or dates or any other weights.

We could live in a type-tagged universe of values. Tags act as types by forming a set of tagged items which means that they are related to the tag and therefore to each other.

Different kinds of items loosely correspond to type-classes, or kinds of types.

Multiple sorted lists are good-enough for everything, and there is org-mode for that.

org-mode provides structuring and nesting (which people call “outlining”) - the fundamental, universal notions . It also provides basic categorization mechanisms and semantic tags.

Yes, you have to develop a habit of using simple sorted nested lists and minimal tools, which are parts of the org-mode package. The goal is to build a nested hierarchical strures of texts which corresponds to conceptual hierarchies of our mind and systems.

Notice that org keywords with "|" are essentially union types, and also a sequence of intermediate states.

They are the most powerful when defined in the “header” on a per-file basis. Thus is a basic typing or a type discipline for entries.

The most difficult thing is to distill the essential workflow from an infinite number of possibilities to mess everything up with useless, redundant micro-management and an illusion of being “optimal”.

So, we write lists - PLANs and TODOs.

PLANs are more general and more about regular activities. A PLAN may include recurring activities. You may state “running every other day” in a PLAN. Or you may say “finish the Okasaki book”.

TODOs are about particular sequences of tasks to be done. You may write “implement this or that” in a TODO.
